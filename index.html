Methodology

An Eight-Stage
NLP Pipeline

From raw headlines to policy recommendations — each stage builds on the last to produce a multi-layered behavioral map of AI discourse.

STAGE 01
Semantic Embeddings
all-MiniLM-L6-v2
Each headline → 384-dimensional vector. Captures meaning beyond keywords: "AI threatens jobs" ≈ "Automation displaces workers."
STAGE 02
K-Means Clustering
k=3, silhouette=0.031
Partitions headlines into 3 macro behavioral domains by minimizing within-cluster variance. Optimal k selected via silhouette scoring.
STAGE 03
VADER Sentiment
VADER Lexicon
Per-headline sentiment scoring. Reveals which domains carry negative vs. optimistic baseline discourse across all 10,500 headlines.
STAGE 04
TF-IDF Keywords
c-TF-IDF
Class-based TF-IDF extracts top 20 signature terms per cluster — the words that define each domain's discourse fingerprint.
STAGE 05
BERTopic Sub-themes
UMAP → HDBSCAN
Within each macro-cluster, discovers granular sub-topics. Healthcare splits into mental health, cardiovascular, and general care concerns.
STAGE 06
Agency Framing
BART-large-MNLI
Zero-shot classification into Active / Passive / Contested frames. Measures whether headlines position humans as agents or subjects of AI.
STAGE 07
Emotion Profiling
NRC Emotion Lexicon
Scores 8 emotions (fear, trust, anticipation, sadness, joy, anger, surprise, disgust) per sub-topic to reveal the emotional texture of AI discourse.
STAGE 08
LLM Recommendations
Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct
Open-weights LLM generates policy recommendations from cluster context. Deterministic (temperature=0) for reproducibility.

Key Results

Three Domains.
Three Different Realities.

AI is transforming human behavior — but not equally. Each domain carries a distinct emotional signature, agency profile, and policy urgency.

12% of headlines
Healthcare
The smallest cluster, yet the most alarming. Patients face 91.8% passive framing in cardiovascular AI coverage. Life-and-death stakes drive the highest behavioral anxiety in the entire dataset.
78.2 Concern / 100
Critical
Immediate action needed. Mandate human-in-the-loop overrides and establish patient AI rights frameworks.
41% of headlines
Education
The most optimistic domain. Lowest fear (0.099), highest joy (0.108). Students and teachers engage with AI willingly — but growing chatbot dependency is quietly shifting agency.
49.6 Concern / 100
Act Now
Growing chatbot passivity means the window for constructive policy is closing. Proactive literacy frameworks are needed.
47% of headlines
Business & Economy
Dominates the conversation yet scores lowest. Workers are afraid, but they are actively demanding governance. Fear paired with agency is manageable — and even productive.
13.7 Concern / 100
Monitor
Channel civic engagement into policy. Prioritize worker reskilling programs and algorithmic accountability auditing.
Counterintuitive Finding

Business dominates 47% of headlines yet scores lowest. Healthcare covers only 12% yet scores 6× higher. Volume of coverage does not equal urgency of intervention. The quietest crisis is the most dangerous one.
